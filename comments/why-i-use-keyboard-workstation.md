**Asep Bagja Priandana** · 2023-04-01 ↩ *reply to RG X*

Here's my review and insight after 1,5 year owning a Korg Kross 2. The sequencer in Kross 2 is indeed useful but only for sketching the song idea or playing a backing track. There's nothing beat the easiness of editing midi track on DAW in laptop, because on DAW you get the visual and can easily drag/drop something with mouse. I can't speak about high end workstation keyboard like Roland Fantom or Korg Nautilus that has bigger screen.

So, I mainly use my Kross 2 only for its sound. Editing its program or combi. When I use internal Kross sequencer for recording a song, then at the end of the process I will export its MIDI file and transfer it to Logic Pro X in my laptop. In Logic Pro there's a capability to play MIDI song but use the sound of external synth hardware (send MIDI out from Logic), in this case my Kross 2. I don't think Garage Band has this feature. With that process in Logic, I can easily edit MIDI: quantise and adding automation to control all parameter in Kross 2. Then, after that I can render this track into WAV and do the mixing.

For performance purpose without a laptop, then you can transfer back any audio files into Kross pad sample. One pad can load 14 seconds audio, so I think you have to prepare the audio as a loop.

In summary, midi controller or Kross 2?
- Choose midi controller if you want work with VST/AU plugin instrument. Also for performance with laptop, you can buy Apple MainStage app it costs only $30.
- Choose Kross 2 if you don't want to bother with VST/AU plugin and doing a performance without laptop. Since you mention your DAW is Garage Band, so you can only export WAV file to GB and mix it. You can't send midi out to play the midi file and send any automations from your GB back to Kross.

 However, Kross also can be used as a midi controller for your GB. Hope this answer help.
